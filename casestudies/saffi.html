Creating an online presence for a clothing boutique

Brief

Saffi Boutique only had an online presence on Facebook, until they requested a brochure website that was strictly non ecommerce. Their goal was to get people to visit their shop for events and personal shopping.

Visit the website

Research

I used the search term "ladies fashion boutique" to look at competitor's sites and see what design trends they were following, and where they were placing their key CTAS.


  • Nav must be clear to use and showcase all the clothing categories.
  • Striking hero image, with CTA on top will encourage users to click.
  • Textures look stylish, overlayed with images to help build the brand.
  • Grid layout looks clean.
  • Gold gives a luxurious feel to a brand.
Mood board

User personas

From conversations with the client and user reviews on Saffi Boutique's Facebook page, I was able to create the following personas.


  • Users like to keep up to date with the latest fashion trends, I ensured the site looked modern.
  • Their key pain point was bad customer service, I highlighted that the team are friendly.
  • Key empathy findings were, users needed to shop somewhere child friendly and free of judgement. The copy in the website reflects that.
User personas

Data

  • As their existing client base access the boutique on their smart phones, I felt it was important for the site to function well across all devices, so I designed it mobile first.
  • indings from heat maps showed users normally click a call to action in the hero section, so this led me to place the "new in" button on the hero image.
  • Using scroll maps, we found on our other sites users clicked off at the end of the product section. This made me come to the decision to place buckets at the bottom of the page, so users had somewhere to navigate to next.

Sitemap

From planning my user's navigation through the site, I was able to come up with a sitemap.


  • I communicated with the client to find out what clothing they want to add to the website, from looking at competitor's sites I decided to place them under the clothing parent page.
  • New in was also an important page, as competitor's used this page to place the items they just recieved so the customer could get quick access to it.
  • I added "boutique" "event days" "about us" "contact us" so the user could see it was more than just a shop. Saffi expressed they wanted to add their own content to event days as they hold events regularly.
